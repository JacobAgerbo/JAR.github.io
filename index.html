Understanding the interactions
between host and microbe

Studying the host-microbe interactions can provide insights into developing novel biotechnologies for disease diagnosis, therapeutics, and engineering microbial communities for industrial and environmental applications.

Unravelling Omics for
metagenomic biodiscovery

I am using massive metagenomic datasets to investigate all
facets of biotech-related microbiology, including secondary metabolites,
antibiotic resistance, and potential phage-therapy candidates

Global biomonitoring
of the little things

Global biomonitoring of bacteria can provide valuable insights into the distribution, diversity, and evolution of bacterial communities across different environments. This information can be used in biotech to develop novel strategies for manipulating microbial communities in ways that are beneficial for human health and the environment.

Main scientific goals

  • Expanded Understanding

    Curiosity is my driving force in science, as it fuels my desire to seek out new knowledge and understanding. By embracing curiosity, I push the boundaries of existing knowledge, challenge assumptions, and discover new insights that can lead to breakthrough discoveries

  • Popularize Advanced Analysis

    Computational analysis can enhance our understanding of microbial communities by providing insights into their diversity, functional potential, and interactions. By using computational tools, you can develop more efficient and accurate methodologies for studying microbiomes.

  • Systematic Data Distillation

    Biological omic dataset needs to be distilled through bioinformatics to gain a deeper understanding of complex biological systems, identify patterns and relationships in large datasets, and develop novel approaches for solving biological problems.

  • Increased Translational Science

    Through my scientific carrier I try to bridge the gap between my research and biotech applications, while maintaining my basic research curiosity.
